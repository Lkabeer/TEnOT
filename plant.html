How to move your plant?

Tips for Transplanting Your Plants

For those hardworking green thumbs out there, it might be important to transplant your garden into your new home. Moving plants can take some time and foresight, but can be done quite easily if planned well ahead of the move. For those traveling abroad, it is unlikely you will be able to take plants with you, given the rules and regulations of customs and immigration. TE&OT provides six tips on moving your plants with as little damage and stress as possible.

  1. The first step is to fully understand the new climate you will be living in, and picking the plants that are most suitable to thrive in this environment. Survey your new home. Does it have enough garden space? Shade? Are the indoor spaces suitable for indoor plants? Are there enough windows? Is there enough sunlight filtering in from the windows. This needs to be worked out before the moving process.
  2. This is where foresight and planning comes into play. In order to successfully transplant plants, it might be necessary to dig up plants well head of the move, several months if possible. You can re-pot these plants, using good plant compost, keeping the soil moist, but not overly wet. Make sure that when you are digging up your plants, you damage the roots as little as possible, and once they are in the “moving pots,” disrupt them as little as possible.
  3. On moving day, remember to remove excess water from the pot. Put the plants in cardboard boxes and line breakable pots with newspaper. For larger pots, it might be better to put them inside plastic shopping bags to keep the soil from spilling over the edges. Make sure to keep the tops of the boxes open, and let those moving your house know that there are fragile plants in these boxes. If you need packing materials for your plants, such as plastic and boxes, let your TE&OT consultant know. We are happy to provide these materials to you at an affordable cost.
  4. Transplanting trees that have been established in the landscape, can prove tricky. TE&OT encourages families to leave trees where they are to avoid damage. But for those who are interested in moving trees, it must be done with immense care and patience. It’s important to root prune a tree in the fall, for a possible move in the spring. If possible, root prune your tree as soon as possible, and well before the move. When moving, make sure to soak the root ball in water, and plant it in a hole that is comparable to the one it had been growing in.
  5. Upon arrival to your new home, it is best to settle in right away. If possible plant your plants as soon as possible. If that is not possible, dig a hole to cover the roots to protect them from colder weather conditions (i.e. frosts). If the ground is already frozen, then keep them in their pots until more suitable weather prevails at your new home.
  6. For indoor plants, keep them wrapped in newspaper (if you have wrapped them in such a manner), until the moving is complete, and the house is closed against the elements, especially if your move takes place during winter. The newspaper insulates the plant from the colder weather conditions.
