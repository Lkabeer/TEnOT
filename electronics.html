Tips to Keeping Your Electronics Safe during the Move

Moving electronics is not only difficult, it can be a stressful process, making sure all the delicate parts are accounted for and taken care of. TE&OT is here to help our clients move their electronics including, telephones, TV’s, gaming equipment, speakers, laptops, desktops, among other devices, with ease and reliability. We will send a consultant to your home to help develop a strategic plan for moving your electronic items. TE&OT also provides the six tips below to help your move your electronics to your new destination.

  1. It’s always best to follow the manufacturer’s instructions for storing/moving the devices. Consult the manual that came with the device, or look on the web to see what packing materials are needed and how to deconstruct, if necessary, any pieces for the big move.   
  2. It’s best to try to save and pack the devices in their original boxes and packing materials. If this is not possible, find the closest boxes or packing materials to the size of the device and use this as a stand-in. Use unprinted newspaper or anti-static bubble wrap to fill in the crevices. Label all your electronics and make a list, so you can keep track of everything, in case something gets lost. TE&OT can provide our clients with packing materials that fit the needed dimensions, including bubble wrap, tape and newspaper.
  3. For those dismantling televisions or desktop computers, make sure you mark the cords, either with colored tape or some other adhesive. Coordinate the colors from the cords with the plugs they go into. This way you can avoid confusion when setting up these devices in your new home.
  4. Use static-free bags or packing materials, such as anti-static bubble wrap and packing peanuts (usually the color pink) to pack all of the wiring/cords and devices. You can also use unprinted newspaper and tissue paper. If not packed with anti-static materials like the ones listed above, your electronic devices can be damaged in the moving process.
  5. Create a “nest” of the unprinted newspaper, tissue paper or other packing materials for the devices to sit in, creating a uniform layer of protection, surrounding all electronics. Seal the box and write “fragile” on the top of it.

Bonus Tip: Remember to remove ink cartridges from all printers before placing them in the box for moving.
